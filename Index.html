Complete Cannabis Cultivation Guide

Cannabis Growth Stages

1. Germination

Duration: 24-72 hours

Light

No direct light needed. Keep seeds in a dark, warm place.

Water

Keep the growing medium moist but not soaked. Use a spray bottle for gentle watering.

Soil

Use a light, airy seed-starting mix or a soilless medium like rockwool cubes. Ensure good drainage to prevent damping off.

Additional Care

Maintain temperature between 70-90°F (21-32°C). Use a humidity dome or plastic wrap to keep moisture in.

2. Seedling Stage

Duration: 2-3 weeks

Light

18-24 hours of light per day. Use T5 fluorescent lights or LED grow lights with a blue spectrum (5000-6500K). Keep lights 2-4 inches from the top of the plants.

Water

Water when the top inch of soil feels dry. Be careful not to overwater. Use room temperature water and water around the base of the plant.

Soil

Transplant into a light, well-draining potting mix. A mix of peat moss, perlite, and compost works well. Ensure pH is between 6.0-6.5.

Additional Care

Maintain temperature between 70-85°F (21-29°C) and humidity around 65-70%. Provide gentle air circulation with a small fan.

3. Vegetative Stage

Duration: 3-16 weeks

Light

18-24 hours of light per day. Use full-spectrum LED grow lights or a combination of Metal Halide (MH) and High-Pressure Sodium (HPS) lights. Increase light intensity as plants grow. Keep lights 12-24 inches from canopy.

Water

Water deeply when the top 2-3 inches of soil are dry. Increase watering as the plant grows larger. Consider using nutrients with each watering, following a 'feed, feed, flush' cycle.

Soil

Use a nutrient-rich potting mix or start adding vegetative nutrients to your basic potting mix. Maintain pH between 6.0-6.5 for soil, 5.5-6.0 for hydroponic setups.

Additional Care

Maintain temperature between 70-85°F (21-29°C) during the day and slightly cooler at night. Keep humidity around 50-70%. Start training techniques like topping or LST (Low Stress Training) to control growth and increase yield.

4. Flowering Stage

Duration: 6-8 weeks

Light

12 hours of light and 12 hours of uninterrupted darkness per day. Use High-Pressure Sodium (HPS) lights or full-spectrum LEDs with enhanced red spectrum (2700-3000K). Gradually increase distance between lights and canopy to 18-24 inches.

Water

Gradually reduce watering frequency but maintain soil moisture. Allow the soil to dry more between waterings as harvest approaches. Switch to flowering nutrients and reduce feeding in the last 2-3 weeks (flushing).

Soil

Continue using the same soil, but switch to bloom nutrients high in phosphorus and potassium. Maintain pH between 6.0-6.5 for soil, 5.5-6.0 for hydroponic setups.

Additional Care

Lower temperature to 65-80°F (18-26°C) and reduce humidity to 40-50%. Monitor for hermaphroditism and remove any male flowers immediately. Consider defoliation to improve light penetration and air circulation.

5. Harvesting and Curing

Duration: 1-3 weeks for drying, 2-8 weeks for curing

Light

No light needed during drying. During curing, store in a dark place to preserve cannabinoids and terpenes.

Water

Stop watering 1-3 days before harvest. No water needed during drying and curing.

Post-Harvest Soil

After harvesting, you can reuse the soil for your next grow. Flush it with pH-balanced water, add new amendments, and let it rest for a few weeks before reusing.

Drying and Curing

Dry in a dark room at 60-70°F (15-21°C) with 45-55% humidity for 7-14 days. Cure in airtight glass jars for at least 2-4 weeks, opening daily to release moisture and exchange air.

Setting Up Your Grow Space

Drain Bucket System

A proper drain bucket system is crucial for managing excess water and maintaining optimal root health.

  1. Choose buckets: Select food-grade 5-gallon buckets with tight-fitting lids.
  2. Drill holes: Create drainage holes in the bottom of each plant bucket.
  3. Set up catch buckets: Place a second bucket underneath to catch runoff.
  4. Install pump: Use a small submersible pump in the catch bucket to recirculate or dispose of runoff.
  5. Monitoring: Regularly check runoff pH and EC (Electrical Conductivity) to adjust feeding.

Tip: Consider using a flood table or trays to manage multiple plants more efficiently.

Plant Nutrition

Essential Nutrients

Cannabis requires a balance of macronutrients and micronutrients throughout its life cycle:

Nutrient Role Deficiency Symptoms
Nitrogen (N) Leaf growth, chlorophyll production Yellowing of older leaves
Phosphorus (P) Root development, flower formation Purple stems, slow growth
Potassium (K) Overall plant health, stress resistance Brown spots, curling leaf edges
Calcium (Ca) Cell wall strength Distorted new growth, tip burn
Magnesium (Mg) Chlorophyll production Interveinal chlorosis

Feeding Schedule

Adjust your feeding schedule based on the plant's growth stage:

  • Seedling: Use 1/4 strength nutrients
  • Vegetative: Gradually increase to full strength, focus on nitrogen
  • Flowering: Reduce nitrogen, increase phosphorus and potassium
  • Late Flowering: Begin flushing 1-2 weeks before harvest

Always start with less and gradually increase. Monitor plant response and adjust accordingly.

Root Care and Maintenance

Root Rinsing

Root rinsing helps prevent nutrient buildup and maintains healthy root systems:

  1. Frequency: Every 2-3 weeks or when changing nutrient solutions
  2. Process:
    • Gently remove plant from its container
    • Rinse roots with pH-balanced water (6.0-6.5)
    • Be gentle to avoid damaging delicate root hairs
    • Reinsert plant into fresh growing medium or cleaned hydroponic system
  3. Benefits: Prevents salt buildup, reduces risk of root rot, refreshes root zone

Note: Root rinsing is more common in hydroponic systems but can benefit soil-grown plants too.

Pest Control

Dealing with Fungus Gnats

Fungus gnats are common pests that thrive in moist environments and feed on organic matter in the soil.

Prevention:

  • Avoid overwatering
  • Use well-draining soil mix
  • Keep grow area clean
  • Use yellow sticky traps to monitor populations

Treatment:

  1. Biological control: Introduce beneficial nematodes or predatory mites
  2. Diatomaceous earth: Apply a thin layer on soil surface
  3. Neem oil: Apply as a soil drench and foliar spray
  4. Hydrogen peroxide solution: Mix 1 part 3% H2O2 with 4 parts water, apply as a soil drench

Always use integrated pest management (IPM) techniques for long-term control.

Advanced Lighting Guide

Types of Grow Lights

Light Type Pros Cons Best Use
Fluorescent (CFL, T5) Low heat, energy-efficient, good spectrum Limited penetration, not ideal for flowering Seedlings, clones, small grows
HID (MH, HPS) High output, good penetration High heat, energy-intensive Large grows, flowering stage
LED Energy-efficient, low heat, customizable spectrum Higher initial cost All growth stages, especially with full-spectrum models
Ceramic Metal Halide (CMH) Full spectrum, energy-efficient Can be expensive, less common All-in-one solution for small to medium grows

Light Spectrum and Cannabis Growth

Different light spectrums promote various aspects of plant growth:

  • Blue light (400-500nm): Promotes vegetative growth, compact plants
  • Green light (500-600nm): Penetrates canopy, supports overall growth
  • Red light (600-700nm): Crucial for flowering and bud development
  • Far-red light (700-750nm): Influences flowering time, stretch
  • UV light (280-400nm): May increase trichome production

Spectrum Recommendations:

  • Seedlings/Clones: Higher blue content (6500K)
  • Vegetative: Full spectrum with emphasis on blue (5000-6500K)
  • Flowering: Full spectrum with increased red (2700-3500K)

Modern LED grow lights often offer customizable spectrums to optimize growth at each stage.
